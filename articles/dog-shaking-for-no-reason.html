Dog Shaking for No Reason

By Animals World  |  Dog Health  |  7 min read

😰 A trembling dog — shaking can have many causes

You notice your dog trembling — a slight, persistent shake that you can't explain. They haven't been wet, it's not cold, and nothing obvious has happened to frighten them. It's unsettling to watch, and it's natural to worry. Trembling in dogs can range from completely benign to a sign of something that needs veterinary attention. Here's how to tell the difference.

Cold and Physical Discomfort

The simplest explanation first: small dogs, dogs with short coats, elderly dogs, and dogs with low body fat can become genuinely cold in temperatures that seem comfortable to you. Chihuahuas, Greyhounds, Italian Greyhounds, and other lean or small-bodied breeds are particularly susceptible. If your dog is shaking in a cool room or after coming inside from cold weather, they may simply need warmth — a blanket, a dog coat, or a spot closer to a heat source.

If warming them up resolves the shaking, that was the cause. If the shaking continues even after they're clearly warm, something else is going on.

Anxiety and Fear

Trembling is a very common physical response to anxiety or fear in dogs. Thunderstorms, fireworks, car rides, veterinary visits, strangers in the home, or any situation that triggers stress can cause a dog to shake visibly. The trembling is driven by the same adrenaline response that makes humans' hands shake when they're nervous.

Other signs that anxiety is the cause include: yawning excessively, panting, pacing, drooling, tucked tail, and seeking closeness with their owner. See our guide to calming an anxious dog for practical strategies.

Pain

A dog in pain often trembles. Pain can originate from many sources — joint inflammation, an injury, abdominal pain, or a dental problem. Dogs are generally stoic about pain (an evolutionary trait from when showing weakness was dangerous), so trembling may be one of the few outward signs that something hurts.

Signs that pain may be behind the shaking include: reluctance to move or be touched in certain areas, changes in posture, loss of appetite, vocalizing when moving or handled, and general withdrawal. If you suspect pain, contact your vet.

Nausea

Dogs often tremble when they're nauseous — before vomiting or even without vomiting. Car sickness, eating something disagreeable, or an underlying digestive issue can all cause nausea-related shaking. If the trembling resolves after your dog vomits or passes time without eating anything suspicious, nausea was likely the cause.

Toxin Ingestion

Certain toxins cause trembling as a primary symptom. These include:

  • Xylitol (found in sugar-free gum and some peanut butters)
  • Chocolate, especially dark chocolate
  • Macadamia nuts
  • Certain mushrooms
  • Some medications (especially human medications)
  • Insecticides and rodenticides

If you suspect your dog has ingested a toxin and is trembling, this is an emergency. Contact your vet or an animal poison control hotline immediately.

Neurological Conditions and Generalized Tremor Syndrome

In some dogs — particularly small breeds like Maltese and West Highland White Terriers — a condition called Generalized Tremor Syndrome (sometimes called "White Shaker Dog Syndrome") causes full-body trembling with no other clear cause. It's an inflammatory condition that responds well to treatment with corticosteroids when properly diagnosed.

Other neurological causes of trembling include epilepsy, degenerative nerve diseases, and spinal issues. These typically present with other neurological signs such as weakness, stumbling, or behavioral changes.

When you see your dog shaking, take a moment to assess the context: are they cold? Frightened? Have they eaten anything unusual? If the shaking is prolonged, severe, accompanied by other symptoms, or you simply can't identify a benign cause, a vet call is the right move. Your instincts about your own dog are valuable — trust them.

Note: If your dog is shaking and you suspect toxin ingestion, seek emergency veterinary care immediately. Do not wait to see if it resolves.
